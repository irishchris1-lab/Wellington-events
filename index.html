Food & Drink

The best family-friendly cafes and restaurants across the Wellington region — spots where whānau are genuinely welcome and everyone can actually enjoy their time.

⭐ Rating:
🌿 whānau:
🍺 Brewery Taproom
Tuatara Brewing — Upper Hutt Taproom
Tuatara's home base in Upper Hutt — one of NZ's most loved craft breweries with a big, relaxed outdoor area that tamariki love to roam around while parents sink a well-earned pint. Rotating food trucks keep everyone fed. A genuinely whānau-welcoming spot with plenty of space, good beer and a great valley vibe.
📍 Brewtown, 16 Miro St, Upper Hutt
☕ Café · Surf Beach
Maranui Café — Lyall Bay
Perched above Lyall Bay's surf beach in the old lifesaving club, Maranui has become one of Wellington's most loved spots. Great coffee, hearty all-day food, and the kind of relaxed vibe that welcomes sandy kids and worn-out parents equally. The beach views from the deck are hard to beat.
📍 2 Marine Parade, Lyall Bay
🦜 Sanctuary Café
Rātā Café — Zealandia
Nestled inside Wellington's world-famous Zealandia sanctuary, Rātā Café is a beautiful spot for coffee and lunch with native birds literally wandering past. A brilliant add-on to a Zealandia visit, or a destination in itself for a peaceful family lunch. Kākā, tūī and kererū are frequent café visitors.
📍 53 Waiapu Rd, Karori, Wellington

Walks & Trails

The best short walks for families with young kids across the Wellington region — all under an hour, mostly flat or gentle, with something interesting to discover along the way.

⏱ Duration: ⭐ Rating:
🌿 Bush Walk
Otari-Wilton's Bush — Circular Walk
⏱ 30–45 min · Easy
NZ's only botanic garden dedicated solely to native plants. The flat lower section of the Circular Walk is perfect for prams and young kids — ancient trees, ferns, birdsong. A genuine bush experience minutes from the city.
📍 160 Wilton Rd, Wilton
🌿 Garden Walk
Wellington Botanic Garden Loop
⏱ 30–60 min · Easy
25 hectares of gardens with pram-friendly paths, a fenced duck pond, native forest section and the beautiful Lady Norwood Rose Garden. Ride the cable car up from Lambton Quay and walk back down. Kids love the sparrows.
📍 Glenmore St, Thorndon
🧚 Fairy Trail
Fairy Forest Trail — Horoeka Reserve
⏱ 20–40 min · Easy
A trail filled with miniature fairy doorways strategically placed along the path — hours of entertainment for young imaginations. One of Wellington's most unique and delightful family walks. Kids will want to do it twice.
📍 Horoeka Reserve, Newlands

Play Parks

The best playgrounds and indoor play centres across the Wellington region — from lighthouse slides on the waterfront to fenced toddler havens and rainy-day escapes.

Wellington City
🛝 Playground · Waterfront
Frank Kitts Park Playground
Wellington's most famous playground — the giant lighthouse slide is an institution. Right on the waterfront with swings, climbing structures, spring ride-ons and usually an ice cream van in summer. The best spot to combine a playground with a harbour walk.
📍 Jervois Quay, Waterfront
🛝 Playground · Garden
Wellington Botanic Garden Playground
One of Wellington's best — fully fenced, newly upgraded in 2024 with a dual flying fox, large climbable tower and three long pipe slides through native bush. Shade sails, sandpit and beautiful garden surroundings. Combine with the cable car for a full day out.
📍 Glenmore St, Thorndon
🛝 Playground · Adventure
Khandallah Park Playground
A hidden gem with incredible panoramic views over Wellington Harbour. Exciting adventure structure with slides, climbing walls and monkey bars, plus a dedicated toddler area. Backs onto native bush — you'll often see kākā from Zealandia. A local favourite.
📍 Khandallah Park, Khandallah
