About this guide

Built with love by Chris, Ash and their family — a Wellington whānau who know how hard it can be to find great things to do with young kids on the weekend.

Our mission is simple: help families across the Wellington region discover brilliant experiences for their tamariki, whether that's a waterfront festival, a hidden fairy trail, or just a great café with a playground out back.

Updated regularly. If you spot something missing or wrong, we'd love to hear from you.

ANZAC Day 2026 — Lest We Forget

Wellington hosts the national ANZAC Day commemorations at Pukeahu. Dawn service at 6am, national ceremony at 11am. Warriors play the ANZAC match that evening.

Saturday 25 April — ANZAC Day
🌺 National Commemoration
ANZAC Day — Dawn Service & National Ceremony
Free
🕐Dawn Service 6:00am · National Ceremony 11:00am
📍Pukeahu National War Memorial Park, Wellington
Wellington hosts New Zealand's national ANZAC Day commemorations. The 6am dawn service at Pukeahu is a deeply moving experience. The 11am National Commemoration Service is broadcast live on TVNZ 1 and RNZ. Also: wreath laying at the Cenotaph, Lambton Quay at 9am. Free and open to all.
Find out more ↗
🏛 Free Exhibition
Te Papa: Gallipoli — The Scale of Our War
Free Family
🕐Open daily · ANZAC weekend especially popular
📍Te Papa Tongarewa, Cable Street, Wellington
Weta Workshop's extraordinary Gallipoli exhibition presents the human story of the campaign through giant-scale figures and personal stories. Deeply moving and perfectly timed for ANZAC Day. Free entry. One of the most powerful museum experiences in New Zealand.
Find out more ↗
🏉 NRL ANZAC Round
NZ Warriors v Dolphins — ANZAC Day Match
Family 🔥 Pick
🕐Saturday 25 April · Kickoff 8:00pm · Gates open 6pm
📍Hnry Stadium, Waterloo Quay, Wellington
💰Ticketed
Wellington hosts the prestigious NRL ANZAC Round for the first time — a historic night with the Warriors facing the Dolphins, including a pre-game ANZAC ceremony. Only the fourth time the Warriors have played an ANZAC match on New Zealand soil. A brilliant way to finish ANZAC Day.
